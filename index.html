1. Introduction

Welcome to Bibliophile ("we," "our," or "us"). Bibliophile is a mobile application that provides a personal library manager, digital bookstore, book marketplace, reading clubs, AI-powered recommendations, and reading statistics. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our mobile application.

We respect your privacy and are committed to protecting your personal data in accordance with applicable international, regional, and local privacy laws. Please read this policy carefully. By using Bibliophile, you consent to the practices described herein.

3.3 Location Data

Bibliophile may request access to your device's location only to show nearby bookstores and sellers on the map feature. Location data is:

  • Requested only with your explicit permission
  • Used only in real-time (not stored on our servers)
  • Never shared with third parties
  • Can be revoked at any time in your device settings

We do NOT collect:

4. How We Use Your Data

6. Data Storage & Security

  • Cloud storage: Account data is stored in Firebase (Google Cloud) with encryption at rest and in transit (TLS 1.3)
  • Local storage: Library data, reading sessions, and preferences are also stored locally on your device using encrypted AsyncStorage
  • Authentication: Passwords are never stored by us — authentication is handled entirely by Firebase Auth with industry-standard hashing
  • API keys: Sensitive API keys are stored securely and never exposed in client-side code bundles

Security Measures

  • Input sanitization on all user-generated content
  • Rate limiting on API requests
  • Firestore security rules with per-user data isolation
  • Error monitoring and anomaly detection
  • Regular security audits and dependency updates

7. Your Rights

7.1 Rights Under GDPR (European Economic Area)

If you are in the EEA, you have the following rights under the General Data Protection Regulation:

  • Right of Access (Art. 15) — Request a copy of all personal data we hold about you
  • Right to Rectification (Art. 16) — Correct inaccurate personal data
  • Right to Erasure (Art. 17) — Request deletion of all your personal data ("Right to be Forgotten")
  • Right to Restriction (Art. 18) — Restrict processing of your data
  • Right to Data Portability (Art. 20) — Receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format
  • Right to Object (Art. 21) — Object to processing based on legitimate interest
  • Right to Withdraw Consent (Art. 7) — Withdraw consent at any time
  • Right to Lodge a Complaint — With your local Data Protection Authority (DPA)

7.2 Rights Under CCPA/CPRA (California, USA)

If you are a California resident:

  • Right to Know — What personal information we collect and why
  • Right to Delete — Request deletion of your personal information
  • Right to Opt-Out — Of the sale of personal information (we do NOT sell your data)
  • Right to Non-Discrimination — We will not discriminate against you for exercising your rights
  • Right to Correct — Inaccurate personal information
  • Right to Limit Use — Of sensitive personal information

California Disclosure: Bibliophile does NOT sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties. We do not engage in "sharing" of personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising as defined by the CPRA.

7.3 Rights Under LGPD (Brazil)

If you are in Brazil, under the Lei Geral de Proteção de Dados:

  • Confirmation of data processing
  • Access to your data
  • Correction of incomplete or inaccurate data
  • Anonymization, blocking, or deletion of unnecessary data
  • Data portability
  • Information about shared data with third parties
  • Revocation of consent

7.4 Rights Under Latin American Laws

Mexico (LFPDPPP): You have ARCO rights — Access, Rectification, Cancellation, and Opposition regarding your personal data.

Argentina (Ley 25.326): You have the right to access, rectify, update, and delete your personal data. You may contact the Dirección Nacional de Protección de Datos Personales.

Chile (Ley 19.628): You have the right to access, modify, cancel, and block your personal data.

9. Data Retention

  • Account data: Retained while your account is active
  • Library & reading data: Retained while your account is active
  • Chat messages: Retained for 2 years, then automatically deleted
  • Marketplace listings: Retained for 1 year after completion/expiry
  • Analytics data: Anonymized and aggregated, retained for 26 months
  • After account deletion: All personal data is permanently deleted within 30 days. Anonymized analytics may be retained.

11. Children's Privacy

Bibliophile is not directed to children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child has provided us with personal data, please contact us immediately at bibliophile.app.contact@gmail.com and we will delete such information within 48 hours.

For users between 13-16 years of age in the EEA, parental consent is required as per GDPR Article 8.

12. Cookies & Tracking

Bibliophile is a mobile application and does not use browser cookies. We use:

  • AsyncStorage: Local device storage for preferences and cached data (not shared externally)
  • Firebase Analytics: Anonymous usage patterns (can be disabled in app settings)

We do not use advertising trackers, fingerprinting, or cross-app tracking technologies.

13. Push Notifications

Bibliophile uses push notifications exclusively for:

  • Reading reminders (user-configured)
  • Reading goal alerts

Push notifications are:

  • Entirely opt-in (never enabled without your permission)
  • Configurable (frequency, time of day)
  • Can be disabled at any time in app settings or device settings

14. AI-Powered Features

Bibliophile offers AI-powered book recommendations using Google's Gemini API. When using this feature:

  • Only book metadata (titles, authors, genres) from your library is sent to the AI
  • No personal identifiers are transmitted
  • AI processing occurs in real-time and data is not retained by the AI service beyond the request
  • You can choose to use local (on-device) recommendations instead of AI at any time
  • AI features can be completely disabled via the in-app toggle
